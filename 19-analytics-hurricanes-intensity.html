Hurricane analytics: intensity

Accumulated cyclone energy

  • Accumulated cyclone energy (ACE) is a metric to express the energy released by a tropical cyclone during its lifetime
  • It is calculating by summing the square of a tropical cyclone’s maximum sustained winds (\(w\)), measured (estimated) every six hours
  • The resulting total is divided by 10,000 to make it more manageable
  • \(\text{ACE} = 10^{-4} \sum w^2\)
  • Summing ACE across each tropical cyclone throughout a season is a measure of total season activity

  • Seasonal ACE values are available from the {hockeystick} package

Per hurricane data

  • The National Hurricane Center has data on all tropical cyclones occurring in the North Atlantic and western North Pacific. These data are available here

  • The International Best-Track Archive for Climate Stewardship has historical tropical cyclone data from around the world in CSV, netCDF, and shapefiles are available. These data are available Here

  • Working with the IBTrACS version 4 data set
  • Here you will:
  1. Download and unzip the data file
  2. Get the data file into R
  3. Glimpse contents

Download and unzip the data file

L <- "https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/data/international-best-track-archive-for-climate-stewardship-ibtracs/v04r00/access/shapefile/IBTrACS.NA.list.v04r00.lines.zip"
  
if(!"IBTrACS.NA.list.v04r00.lines.zip" %in% list.files(here::here("data"))) {
download.file(url = L,
              destfile = here::here("data",
                                    "IBTrACS.NA.list.v04r00.lines.zip"))
unzip(here::here("data", "IBTrACS.NA.list.v04r00.lines.zip"),
      exdir = here::here("data"))
}
