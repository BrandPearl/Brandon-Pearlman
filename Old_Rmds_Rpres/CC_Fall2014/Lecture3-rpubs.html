Global Warming is About Heat

Heat is proportional to the kinetic (motion) energy of the molecules.

\[ \hbox{energy} \sim \frac{d}{2}kT, \] \( T \) is temperature, \( k \) is Boltzmann constant, and \( d \) is the degrees of freedom of the molecule.

Why is CO\( _2 \) a greenhouse gas, but not Argon?

Ar (\( d \) = 3), O\( _2 \)/N\( _2 \) (\( d \) = 6, 1 vibrational, 2 twisting), CO\( _2 \) (\( d \) = 9)

L2 = 'mlost/operational/products/aravg'
L3 = '.ann.land.00N.90N.v3.5.4.201407.asc'
L = paste(L1, L2, L3, sep = "")
df = read.table(L)
names(df) = c("Year", "Temperature", "Anom")
df[1:3, 1:2]

Plot Temperature Data

library(ggplot2)
ggplot(df, aes(x = Year, y = Temperature)) +
  geom_line()

Improve Plot

ggplot(df, aes(x = Year, y = Temperature)) +
  geom_line() + geom_smooth() +
  ylab("Temperature Anomaly (C)")

Not Just the Average

Temperature distribution, exceedance frequency, correlation.

ggplot(df, aes(Temperature)) +
  geom_histogram(binwidth = .25, fill = "red") +
  xlab("Temperature Anomaly (C)")
